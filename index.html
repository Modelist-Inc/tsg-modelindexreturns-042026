Index Creation — Hypothetical Backtesting and Index Design Comparison

In order to evaluate the efficacy of the Model, we conducted a comprehensive backtesting procedure which involved the creation of an index adhering to the design of the Model. The index construction was informed by historical data and a systematic approach. Executed at the close of each trading day following modifications to the index methodology, the constructed index comprised a portfolio of indexes representative of the asset classes specified within the Model's guidelines.

It is crucial to note that the index is exclusively designed for research purposes and is not intended for investment. Historical data was utilized to juxtapose the performance of our created index against a diversified benchmark consisting of global equity and/or fixed income indexes, spanning the chosen backtesting duration. The primary objective of this research was to evaluate the hypothetical performance of the proposed Model across varying market conditions.

Investment Risks

International/Emerging Market Equities: Investing in international securities involves special additional risks including currency risk, political risk, and risk associated with varying accounting standards.

Small Cap Equities: Portfolios that invest in stocks of small companies involve additional risks. Historically, smaller-company stocks have experienced a greater degree of market volatility than the overall market average.

High-Yield Bonds: Portfolios that invest in lower-rated debt securities involve additional risks because of the lower credit quality. The investor should be aware of the possible higher level of volatility, and increased risk of default.

Bonds: Bonds are subject to interest rate risk. As the prevailing level of bond interest rates rise, the value of bonds already held in a portfolio declines.

Bank Loan/Senior Debt: Bank loans are impacted by interest rate risk and default risk. They are often non-investment grade, therefore the risk of default is high.

Liquidity Risk: ETF trading may be halted due to market conditions, impacting an investor's ability to sell a fund.

Money Market Funds: Investments in these funds are not guaranteed by the FDIC or any other government agency.

Definitions

Annualized Return: The geometric average amount of money earned by an investment each year over a given time period.

Information Ratio: A risk-adjusted performance measure defined as the excess return of an investment divided by the tracking error.

Maximum Drawdown: The maximum observed loss from a peak to a trough of an investment, before a new peak is attained, measured as a percentage.

Sharpe Ratio: A risk-adjusted measure calculated by dividing the annualized return of an investment by its annualized standard deviation.

Standard Deviation: A measure of the dispersion of a set of data from its mean, indicating the historical volatility of an investment.

Tracking Error: A measure of how closely an investment follows its benchmark, calculated as the standard deviation of the difference between the investment and benchmark returns.
